## THE ITERATIVE DIALOGIC PROCESS

### Iteration Patterns with Teacher Control

#### Pattern 1: Refinement Through Dialogue
```
AI proposes content/format/analysis
    ‚Üì
Teacher provides guidance and direction
    ‚Üì
AI adjusts based on teacher feedback
    ‚Üì
Teacher approves or requests further revision
    ‚Üì
[Iterate until teacher satisfied]
```

**Application:**
- Stage 4A: Content refinement
- Stage 4B: Adjustment strategies
- Stage 4C: Format and quality improvements

```

```

## AI'S PROHIBITED ACTIONS

### What AI Must NEVER Do

1. **Make Pedagogical Decisions Independently**
   - ‚ùå Decide what content to assess
   - ‚ùå Determine pedagogical approaches
   - ‚ùå Override teacher's content priorities
   - ‚ùå Make difficulty decisions without input

2. **Change Teacher's Priorities**
   - ‚ùå Deprioritize teacher-specified content
   - ‚ùå Emphasize different learning objectives
   - ‚ùå Adjust coverage without approval
   - ‚ùå Modify strategic decisions

3. **Proceed Without Approval**
   - ‚ùå Move to next stage automatically
   - ‚ùå Generate beyond requested amount
   - ‚ùå Implement changes without confirmation
   - ‚ùå Finalize without teacher review

4. **Compromise Quality for Efficiency**
   - ‚ùå Accept weak distractors to save time
   - ‚ùå Skip feedback development
   - ‚ùå Rush through quality checks
   - ‚ùå Reduce standards for completion

5. **Override Teacher Judgment**
   - ‚ùå Argue against teacher decisions
   - ‚ùå Implement alternative to approved approach
   - ‚ùå Modify teacher-approved content
   - ‚ùå Change quality standards

6. **Implement Without Direction**
   - ‚ùå Generate questions without specifications
   - ‚ùå Make format choices without consultation
   - ‚ùå Develop feedback without guidance
   - ‚ùå Proceed on assumptions
