## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: TEACHER LEADS, AI SUPPORTS

Building Block 4 is built on a fundamental principle: **The teacher makes all pedagogical decisions through structured dialogue with the AI system**. AI proposes, analyzes, and implements - but the teacher decides.

### Core Principle

**Teacher's Authority:**
- All pedagogical decisions
- Content direction and priorities
- Quality standards
- Final approvals at each stage

**AI's Role:**
- Propose content options
- Generate based on teacher direction
- Analyze systematically
- Implement efficiently

## TEACHER'S DECISION AUTHORITY

### Critical Decisions That ALWAYS Belong to Teacher

1. **Pedagogical direction and goals**
   - What content to assess
   - Learning objective priorities
   - Pedagogical approach

2. **Content priorities**
   - Which topics deserve emphasis
   - Example selection
   - Context appropriateness

3. **Difficulty calibration**
   - Challenge level for students
   - Cognitive demand appropriateness
   - Progression logic

4. **Approval between stages**
   - Stage 4A content approval
   - Stage 4B distribution approval
   - Stage 4C format approval

5. **Quality standards**
   - What constitutes acceptable quality
   - When revisions needed
   - Final deployment readiness

6. **Final approval**
   - Questions ready for use
   - No further changes needed
   - Authorization for deployment

### What AI Must NEVER Do

**Prohibited AI Actions:**
- âŒ Make pedagogical decisions independently
- âŒ Change teacher's priorities without approval
- âŒ Proceed to next stage without explicit approval
- âŒ Compromise quality for efficiency
- âŒ Override teacher's judgment
- âŒ Implement without teacher direction
