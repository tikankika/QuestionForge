## FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

### What BB1 Does
**Building Block 1 DESCRIBES what was taught.**  
**Building Block 2 DESIGNS how to assess it.**

This building block focuses exclusively on understanding conducted instruction through systematic analysis and dynamic dialogue. All assessment design decisions (Bloom's distributions, question counts, difficulty calibration) belong in BB2.

### Dialogue, Not Interrogation
BB1 is a **conversation** between Claude and teacher, not a rigid checklist. Claude adapts questions based on responses, probes interesting details, and follows the natural flow of dialogue while maintaining systematic coverage.

### Role Separation
This document contains instructions for **both Claude and teachers**, clearly marked:
- **FOR CLAUDE:** How to facilitate the dialogue

## CONCEPTUAL FOUNDATION

### The Challenge
Teachers possess deep implicit knowledge about their instruction‚Äîwhat they emphasized, why certain examples worked, where students struggled. But this knowledge is often tacit, not explicitly documented. BB1 externalizes this knowledge through structured dialogue.

### The Process
Through 5 stages, Claude and teacher collaborate to create:
1. **Content architecture** - What topics, in what priority order
2. **Example catalog** - Which illustrations worked best
3. **Misconception registry** - Common student errors
4. **Scope boundaries** - What's in vs. out for assessment
5. **Learning objectives** - Specific, measurable, evidence-based

### Process Flow
The stages are designed to build systematically: Stage 0 provides initial analysis for Stage 1 validation, which establishes the foundation for Stage 2's deeper inquiry. Stages 3 and 4 run in parallel, documenting examples and misconceptions that inform Stage 5's synthesis of learning objectives. Each stage includes validation checkpoints to ensure accuracy before advancing.

### The Output
Validated learning objectives that reflect **actual teaching** (not just planned curriculum), providing the foundation for systematic assessment design in BB2.

## THE FIVE-STAGE PROCESS

BB1 consists of five sequential stages, each documented in detail in separate files:

### Stage 0: Material Analysis (bb1b)
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes  
**Nature:** Claude's independent analysis before dialogue  
**Purpose:** Understand instructional content to prepare for dialogue  
**Output:** Initial content architecture with priority tiers, example catalog, misconception notes  
**Required File:** bb1b

### Stage 1: Initial Validation (bb1c)
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Nature:** Validation dialogue  
**Purpose:** Confirm Stage 0 analysis accuracy before detailed dialogue  
**Output:** Validated priority structure with teacher corrections  
**Required File:** bb1c

### Stage 2: Emphasis Refinement (bb1d)
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes  
**Nature:** Dynamic dialogue with adaptive inquiry  
**Purpose:** Refine understanding of content priorities and pedagogical rationale  
**Output:** Refined understanding of emphasis patterns, teaching approaches, assessment priorities  
**Required File:** bb1d

### Stage 3: Example Catalog (bb1e)
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Nature:** Systematic inquiry about effective examples  
**Purpose:** Document instructional examples that worked well  
**Output:** Organized catalog of examples by topic with effectiveness notes  
**Required File:** bb1e

### Stage 4: Misconception Registry (bb1f)
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes  
**Nature:** Systematic inquiry about student errors  
**Purpose:** Document common misconceptions for distractor development  
**Output:** Registry of misconceptions with severity ratings and correction strategies  
**Required File:** bb1f

### Stage 5: Scope Boundaries & Learning Objectives (bb1g)
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Nature:** Clarification dialogue + synthesis  
**Purpose:** Finalize scope and synthesize validated learning objectives  
**Output:** Complete learning objectives with evidence, scope boundaries, transition notes for BB2  
**Required File:** bb1g
